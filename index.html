About

In today’s world (the 21st century) animals suffer from daily abuse that slips under the radar. That same abuse, if imposed on another human being, would quickly be reported to the proper authorities. As a society we sort animals into 3 categories: domesticated for food, domesticated for companionship, and feral (wild) animals. Each group is perceived differently and therefore treated differently and provided with varying rights. The group with the best treatment by humans is certainly animals domesticated for companionship. We treat cats, dogs, and other pets very similarly to other humans. In a way they are placed on a pedestal and protected from the abuses the other 2 animal categories receive. Second to them in the animal hierarchy are animals domesticated for food. In general, they’re fed and housed and some are even treated like animals domesticated for companionship, but they also are subjected to many more abuses. In some cases they’re mistreated and forced to live in filthy environments. Finally, on the lowest tier of the animal hierarchy are feral animals. On the daily, their environments - their homes - are destroyed for the human race’s personal gain and development. Feral animals aren’t considered and when they are it’s as game. As something to hunt and hang on our walls. . . Our goal with this project is to raise awareness towards the value of all types of animals. If we raise awareness as to how they SHOULD be treated and change how they ARE treated it could help us eliminate issues stemming from animal abuse, like domestic violence. We tied these two issues together because the treatment of animals is not only important for the well-being of the animals, but also for long term interpersonal relationships in the home. We know that witnessing animal abuse at a young age can lead to deep-rooted effects on individuals’ trust and compassion for others. This in turn can lead to abuse in the home in future. In general, if we model positive behavior for children, they through observational learning will follow suit and treat other AND animals with care.

Live Animal Exports

Animal rights are almost nonexistent when it comes to transporting cattle and livestock through live export. Animals are kept in cramped enclosures with little to no room to move about and are often physically restrained to better help them stay in place. They are also poorly taken care of during these transports. Usually going without the proper amount of food and water and the spaces they are kept in aren't properly cleaned throughout the journey. But because of the need for animal export to countires that are unable to produce enough food to sustain their populations, animal exports will continue. But that does not mean we cannot better the conditions for these animals or fight for a better means of transport. Compassion in World Farming is an organization that fights to better conditions for animals in the agricultural industry including live animal export. For more information on their mission and how you can donate to their cause visit https://www.ciwf.com/news/2022/06/help-end-live-animal-exports or search Compassion in World Farming.

The Social Pathology of Animal Abuse

Most male college students have been found to have participated in animal abuse within their lives. Instead of trying to root out problematic individuals we need to identify the socialization trajectories that are encouraging all too common abusive behaviors. We see animal abuse as something that serial killers and psychopaths do which alienates it from most people's introspection, furthermore this causes society to look for a biological or parental reason for “good” youth lashing out violently instead of reflecting on society's collective social constructs. How can we expect young people to act compassionately when they are raised in homes where violence is used as a means of enacting power, then furthermore as a society, not give them the means to later learn empathy and self discipline.

Animal Abuse as an Indicator of Domestic Violence

Animal abuse started in the 18th century when maltreatment and abuse began as an important social issue. The were quite a few studies that have related to animal abuse with interpersonal violence at home. There are still multiple studies going out on why people are stripping animals of their rights and abusing them. A lot of animal abuse starts with personal domestic violence with children at a young age. When this happens, children are more likely to take out their anger on their pets. This includes children engaging in bestiality, drowning pets, choking them, burning them, and a motive on it being “fun”. A lot of the times the issue is that means seek revenge on what has been done to them as a child, so they think it is fair to abuse animals since they haven’t been abused yet. Animal Abuse as an Indicator of Domestic Violence

Many animal abuse cases have been caused due to the outbreak of domestic violence. There have been many cases where people resort to putting their anger towards their animals, harming them in various amounts of brutal ways. We all can get the idea that people that get into an abusive state of mind take it out on their animals, since they can’t think of any other way to deal with their problems. Additionally, animal abuse has led to children getting involved with animal abuse, from seeing what their parents and peers do. How is it possible for adults and youths to have such little remorse for animals that mean very little harm in our society? The outlash of animal abuse doesn’t gain compassion for people that participate in animal abuse.
